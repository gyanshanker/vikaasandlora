Our Story

Friendship, Relationship

Our relationship is truly one for the 21st Century and beyond.

We met through an online dating app in 2014 called OKCupid. We had our first date on April 3, 2014. Exactly two years later, after learning we were meant for each other through the ups and downs of a relationship, we were engaged.

We had a Hindu ceremony for our engagement in September 2016, and held a beautiful wedding in the auspicious city of Puri, in the state of Odisha in India, with more than 300 of our family and friends.


--Vikaas Shanker and Lora Khuntia

How we met?

Lora had just came back from India in late March of 2014 when she decided to check out her dating profile on OKCupid.com. She saw a neatly worded message from what seemed like a nice guy after some Facebook snooping. Vikaas, who was settling into his first job as a news reporter, also checked out Lora’s profile. He found a beautiful woman who was loving and caring.

We both messaged each other back, and days later set a date. On April 3, 2014, we met at Mama Onesta’s, a small but elegant Italian restaurant in Lockport, Illinois, a midway point between Lora in Mokena, and Vikaas in Plainfield.

After a successful dinner, we had more dates at the movie theater, bowling alley, and we shared our first kiss. When we weren’t meeting each other, we would watch movies through Netflix over the phone. And as our relationship continued, and as we met both of our families, so did our love grow

Our Wedding

We decided to have our wedding in a religious setting in Puri, Odisha, India, on Valentines Day, 2017. A Hindu ceremony was conducted along the coast of the Bay of Bengal. This was important to us because we wanted each grandmother to witness the wedding and give us their blessings. We also thought it was important to celebrate with our families in India.

Wedding Invitation Card

The Venue

The city of Puri is located on the eastern coast of India, in the state of Odisha. Lora’s mother’s family is from Puri, a town of festivals that is considered an auspicious area featuring the famous Jagannath Temple. Lord Jagannath is the ishtavatar, or primary deity, of Lora’s household.

Our Wedding

The wedding festivities were spread across several days, from Feb. 12-14 in 2017. The night of Feb. 12, our extended families in India were introduced to each other in an informal meet-and-greet event. A day later, we held our Sangeet and Mehndi, which is when both families come together once again to enjoy a cultural program of singing and dancing, as well as for women (and some men if they choose) to adorn their hands and feet with mehndi. In the afternoon of Feb. 14, Vikaas rode in on a horse-drawn chariot as his family led him while dancing to music in a ceremony known as the “Baraat.” After Vikaas was escorted to the mandap stage, Lora walked down with her cousin sisters. Later that evening, after the wedding, we held a formal reception to thank all those who came.

Celebration - Clovis, CA

About three months later on May 20, 2017, Vikaas’ parents hosted another celebration for our family and friends in California. Friends and family were entertained with a video of our wedding, as well as speeches and comedy routines from talented individuals. Lora’s parents, and Vikaas’ brother and friends flew in from across the country, and many more drove hundreds of miles to be with us that day.
